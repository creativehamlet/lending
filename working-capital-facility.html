Working Capital Facility

A working capital facility is an ongoing arrangement between the borrower and lender, allowing the business owner to draw funds against a set, maximum loan balance at any time as long as they do not exceed the limit.

Advantages
  • Interest is not charged on the unused portion of the line of credit
  • Adaptable financial planning since funds can be drawn whenever necessary
  • Variable payments once funds are drawn
Disadvantages
  • Up-front costs often associated with obtaining the line
  • Depending on the business structure, an owner may be personally liable for repayment if the business fails
Minimum Requirements
  • 1 year in business
  • $100,000 in annual revenue
Additional Information Requirements
  • Bank statements
  • Financial statements
  • Business tax returns
  • Personal guarantee
