The debt service ratio measures your ability to cover ongoing debt payments.

A debt service coverage ratio of 2 or higher is typically considered good for a small business. You may improve your debt service coverage ratio by increasing your business's operating profit or by reducing debt payments. A poor debt service coverage ratio will greatly reduce your ability to take on additional loans.

The net income to sales ratio measures the profitability of your business.

This ratio measures how much out of every dollar of sales a company actually keeps in earnings. Generally, you want this ratio to be as close to one as possible, as that indicates a lower proportion of expenses. You may improve the ratio by reducing direct and indirect costs of operating the business. Negative net income is viewed upon unfavorably by lenders.

How is the Score Calculates?

The Sageworks Small Business Score is based on a proprietary credit-rating model developed by Sageworks, the leader in the financial analysis of private companies. The model is built through rigorous analysis of how businesses' basic financial and descriptive metrics predicts credit events. The model has three main components:

Financial Performance, Personal Credit-worthiness, and General Business Health

1. We built a quantitative model using Sageworks' database containing thousands of up-to-date private company's financials and industry benchmarks.

2. For small businesses, personal credit scores of the primary borrower is a critical piece to the credit equation. We ask for your credit score and factor it into the Business Credit Score based on the size of the business. Because the score is only calculated for your reference, we do not perform a pull of your actual credit score.

3. Finally, we calculate a qualitative measure of business health based on a few simple questions about your business.
